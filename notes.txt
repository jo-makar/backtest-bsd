# Summary of "The Little Book of Big Dividends" by Charles Carlson
- Dividend yield alone is a dangerous metric
  - High dividend yields are often indicators of distress and signal potential dividend cuts
  - Better to invest in healthy companies with sustainable dividends
- Use a two-factor screening process (ie the Basic BSD Formula)
  - Set upper threshold of 60% of profits consumed by dividend
  - Use a quality filter such as the proprietary Quadrix Score, specifically 75 or higher
    - It ranks a stock based on several metrics' relative performance as a percentile
      - Ie the stock with best metric value gets a 100, the lowest gets a 0
    - Then all the metric percentiles are (weight) averaged and re-ranked for a final score
    - Metric categories: Value, Quality, Momentum, Financial strength, Earnings estimates, Performance

# Simulating the Quadrix Score
- Using 10-Q (Quarterly Progress) and 10-K (Annual Comprehensive) from the SEC
  - Ref: https://www.sec.gov/data-research/sec-markets-data/financial-statement-data-sets
  - Note that the Q1 10-Q reports three months of activity, the Q2 six months and the Q3 nine months

- The category metrics are percentile ranked prior then (weight) averaged
  - In some cases they are first inverted, so higher values are always better values

- Value metrics (weight 25%)
  - P/FCF (Price to Free Cash Flow) (inverted, weight 60%)
    - Price to Free Cash Flow = Market Capitalization / Total Stockholders Equity
    - Market Capitalization = Outstanding Shares * Share Price
    - SEC Tag: StockholdersEquity
  - P/E (Price to Earnings) (inverted, weight 40%)
    - Market Price per Share / Diluated Earnings Per Share
    - SEC Tag: DilutedEarningsPerShare

- Quality metrics (weight 25%)
  - ROE (Return on Equity) (weight 45%)
    - Net Income (TTM) / Total Stockholders Equity
      - TTM: Trailing Twelve Months
    - SEC Tags: NetIncomeLoss, StockholdersEquity
  - ROA (Return on Assets) (weight 35%)
    - Net Income (TTM) / Total Assets
    - SEC Tags: NetIncomeLoss, Assets
  - Operating Margin (weight 20%)
    - Operating Income / Total Revenues
    - SEC Tags: OperatingIncomeLoss, Revenues

- Momentum metrics (weight 20%)
  - Quarterly FCF Growth (YoY) (weight 50%)
    - (Free Cash Flow (Current Quarter) / Free Cash Flow (Same Quarter Prior Year)) - 1
  - Quarterly Operating Income Growth (YoY) (weight 30%)
    - (Operating Income (Current Quarter) / Operating Income (Same Quarter Prior Year)) - 1
  - Quarterly Revenue Growth (YoY) (weight 20%)
    - (Revenue (Current Quarter) / Revenue (Same Quarter Prior Year)) - 1

- Financial strength metrics (weight 10%)
  - Interest Coverage Ratio (weight 60%)
    - Operating Income (EBIT) / Interest Expense
    - SEC Tags: OperatingIncomeLoss / InterestExpense
  - Total Debt to Equity (inverted, weight 40%)
    - (Short-Term Debt + Long-Term Debt) / Total Stockholders Equity
    - SEC Tags: ShortTermBorrowings, LongTermDebt, StockholdersEquity

- Earnings estimates metrics (weight 10%)
  - Sequential Diluted EPS Growth (weight 70%)
    - (Diluted EPS (Current Quarter) / Diluted EPS (Previous Quarter)) - 1
  - Filing Timeliness Index (inverted, weight 30%)
    - Number of days between the quarter end date and SEC report acceptance date
    - SEC Tags: PeriodEndOfReport, FilingDate

- Performance metrics (weight 10%)
  - Price Momentum Correlation (weight 50%)
    - The three-month (weight 30%) and six-month (weight 70%) total returns
  - Book Value to Market Cap Reversion Potential (weight 50%)
    - Book Value to Market Cap Reversion Potential = Stockholders Equity / Market Capitalization
    - Stockholders Equity = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
    - SEC Tag: StockholdersEquity
